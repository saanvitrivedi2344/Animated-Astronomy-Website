O-Type

Hot, blue stars with temperatures over 30,000K. Short-lived but extremely luminous.

B-Type

Blue-white stars with temperatures between 10,000-30,000K. Includes Rigel and Spica.

A-Type

White stars like Sirius and Vega with temperatures 7,500-10,000K.

F-Type

Yellow-white stars like Procyon, slightly hotter than our Sun.

G-Type

Yellow stars like our Sun with temperatures 5,200-6,000K.

K-Type

Orange stars like Alpha Centauri B, cooler than the Sun.

M-Type

Red dwarfs like Proxima Centauri, the most common star type.
